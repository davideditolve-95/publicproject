The Benchmark: COSO 2013

Before analyzing TIM, we must establish our yardstick. The COSO Framework is the global gold standard for internal controls, focusing on three core objectives supported by five integrated components.

⚙

Operations

Ensuring effectiveness and efficiency in daily business operations.

📊

Reporting

Reliability of financial and non-financial data published to stakeholders.

⚖

Compliance

Adherence to applicable laws, regulations, and internal policies.

Three Lines of Defense

TIM's structure ensures clear separation of duties between those who take risks and those who check them.

3rd Line: Assurance Internal Audit (Reports to Board)
2nd Line: Oversight ERM, Compliance, Security Functions
1st Line: Management Operational Managers & Risk Owners

Key Policy Examples

  • Anti-Corruption (ISO 37001) Strict prohibition of "facilitation payments". Tightly regulated gifts.
  • Modello 231 Maps criminal liabilities to activities. Overseen by Supervisory Body (SB).
  • Financial Reporting Dedicated "Officer Responsible" ensures accounting procedure compliance.

Information Flows

Controls are useless if not communicated. TIM manages flow in four critical directions:

↑ Upward/Downward

Executive Directors report quarterly to the Board on operations.

→ Internal

Code of Ethics disseminated via Intranet & mandatory training.

← External Market

Strict Investor Relations protocol for transparency & fairness.

⚠ Whistleblowing

Protected anonymous channels for reporting violations.
